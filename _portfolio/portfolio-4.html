Robotic Construction Automation - ACE Build Automation

Project Overview

Contributed to the development of robotic construction systems at ACE Build Automation (Baumatix), a pioneering company streamlining the design-through-construction process of creating buildings. ACE is revolutionizing the construction industry by developing and implementing robotic systems for automated building manufacturing.

Key Contributions

  • Connector Design & Development: Designed, machined, and prototyped 6 different sizes of plumbing connectors for automated building assembly systems
  • Structural Connector Innovation: Developed and prototyped a novel structural connector system that enables rapid building assembly
  • Patent-Generating Work: Both the plumbing connector system and structural connector designs resulted in granted patents, demonstrating significant technical innovation and intellectual property creation
  • Prototyping & Testing: Led the complete design-to-prototype process, from initial concept through machining and functional testing
  • Manufacturing Integration: Designed connectors specifically for integration with automated building manufacturing processes

Technology Innovation

ACE Build Automation is transforming traditional construction by:

  • Manufacturing buildings in large subsections in controlled factory environments
  • Using powerful automated equipment instead of mobile subpar tools
  • Dramatically reducing construction time while increasing building quality
  • Eliminating waste from subcontractor delays, labor shortages, weather delays, and cost overruns
  • Improving safety by moving construction to controlled factory environments

Technical Expertise

  • Connector Design & Prototyping: Complete design-to-prototype process for plumbing and structural connectors
  • Precision Machining: Hands-on machining and manufacturing of prototype connector systems
  • Patent Development: Technical innovation resulting in granted patents for connector systems
  • Multi-Scale Design: Designing connectors across 6 different size ranges for various building applications
  • Structural Engineering: Advanced structural connector design for rapid building assembly
  • Manufacturing Integration: Designing for automated manufacturing and assembly processes
